Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is GDP and how is it calculated?

    GDP is the total value of all goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific time period. It's calculated using three approaches: production (sum of value added by industries), expenditure (sum of consumption, investment, government spending, and net exports), and income (sum of all incomes earned in the economy).

  • Ho does inflation impacts economies and how is it measured globally?

    Inflation is the increase in general price levels, reducing purchasing power. It can lead to uncertainty and affect economic decisions. Inflation is measured using Consumer Price Index (CPI) or Producer Price Index (PPI), which track changes in prices of goods and services over time.

  • What are the key indicators used assess the health of job market on global scale?

    Key job market indicators include the unemployment rate (percentage of the workforce without jobs), labor force participation rate (percentage of working-age population in the labor force), and employment-to-population ratio (percentage of working-age population that's employed).

  • How is international trade measured,and what are major factors that influence trade balance between countries?

    International trade is measured by the value of goods and services crossing borders. Factors influencing trade balances include exchange rates, trade policies, comparative advantage, and economic conditions in trading partners.

  • What does the PMI olay understanding the manufacturing and service sectors of different economies?

    PMI is an indicator that provides insight into economic health by surveying purchasing managers in manufacturing and services sectors. A PMI above 50 suggests expansion, while below 50 indicates contraction.

  • What is the siginificans of the balance of payments,how does it provides insights in countries?

    The balance of payments is a record of all economic transactions between a country and the rest of the world. It includes the current account (trade in goods and services) and the capital account (financial transactions). It reflects a country's economic relationship with others and its external financial position.
